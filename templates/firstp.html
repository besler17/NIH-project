  Introduction

National Institutes of Health (NIH) is one of the world's foremost medical research centers, an agency of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services making important discoveries that improve health and save lives.

NIH has a budget more than $30 billion a year to enhance life, and reduce illness and disability. NIH funded research has led to breakthroughs and new treatments, helping people live longer, healthier lives, and building the research foundation that drives discovery.

  Date Sources

The NIH funding data is obtained from NIH funding reporter.

The data source of publication information is NCBI through its API.

The impact factor data is from Glassdoor sharing.

  Analytic Methedology
